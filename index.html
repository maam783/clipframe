How it works

1

Drop a video

Pick or drag an MP4, MOV or WebM. It opens instantly — no upload.

2

Trim & tune

Drag the handles to choose a clip, then set FPS, width and colors.

3

Export & share

Get a clean GIF — no watermark — to download or share anywhere.

Genuinely private. Your video is decoded and encoded entirely on your device using your browser's built-in video engine. There is no server, no account, and no tracking — the page can't even make a network request (enforced by its content-security policy).

Made to feel instant. Most GIF makers upload your file, convert it on a server, and send it back. Clipframe skips the round-trip: the work happens locally in a background thread, so a short clip is ready in well under a second on a modern machine.

Privacy & legal

Privacy

Clipframe runs entirely in your browser. The video you choose is never uploaded — it is read from your device, processed locally, and the resulting GIF is created on your machine. We operate no server that receives your files.

We set no cookies and run no analytics or tracking. The only thing stored is your last choice of FPS, width, colors and dither — kept in this browser's localStorage purely so the controls remember your preference. Clearing site data removes it. The page's content-security policy blocks all network connections, so this is verifiable, not just promised.

Open-source libraries

Clipframe bundles gifenc (MIT, © Matt DesLauriers) for GIF encoding and mp4box.js (BSD-3-Clause, © Telecom ParisTech / GPAC) for MP4 demuxing. Their license texts ship in the /vendor folder.
